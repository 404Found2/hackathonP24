Precious Metals and Currencies

Precious Metals-Overview


Although gold and silver are the most common precious metals, there are many more, each with their own purpose. Here are some examples of popular precious metals investors hold as well as the most common reason for doing so:
  • Gold - It’s durable, malleable, and has a long history as a currency and a symbol of wealth. In addition, it can be easily exchanged for liquid cash in economic recessions/depressions.
  • Silver - It has relevance in both currency and industrial uses. Silver is a component in many appliances and (in addition to gold) is often used as an inflation hedge.
  • Platinum - Platinum is one of the rarest precious metals, but it has industrial uses as an electric conductor. Its rarity offers the long term value that most investors hope to find in their investments.
  • Palladium - Palladium’s demand in currencies, dental applications, and jewelry making, and its rarity makes it a valuable asset for investors

Commodity ETFs


Precious metals exchange-traded funds (ETFs) hold assets like gold, silver, and platinum and sell shares in their portfolios. On the other hand, Currency ETFs provide investors with a simple way to invest in foreign exchange markets, where traders bet on the performance of different international currencies.
Pros:
  • Can be source of diversification for your investment portfolio
  • Can be one of the most liquid ways to invest in precious metals and other commodities
Cons:
  • Volatile prices depending on currency it’s traded on and other economic factors
  • Counter-party risk of management fund facing instability or having management issues

Bullion


Bullion is the physical form of precious metals like gold. This can come in many forms such as coins, bars, ingots, rings etc. The value of the bullion comes from the value of the metal itself rather than the use of the object.
Pros:
  • Physically valuable and can be used in times of emergency as a medium of exchange
  • Initial investment security and future growth guarantee
Cons:
  • Presents need to safely store precious metals and avoid theft
  • Prices are often controlled and limited by the largest buyers

Certificates


Precious metals certificates are documents issued by governments or other private entities that certify the holder’s ownership of a specific quantity of precious metals, usually gold or silver.
Pros:
  • More liquid and lower entry costs and buying in smaller quantities is a possibility
  • Convenient and a verified quality of precious metal
Cons:
  • No physical ownership of the gold/No “collectible” value
  • Counterparty risk of issuer not being able to provide the value of that commodity
