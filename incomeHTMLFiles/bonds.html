Bonds and CDs

CDs


CDs, also known as certificates of deposits, are excellent ways to make money. They are protected against risk by the US Government and offer higher interest rates than the average savings account. CDs interest rates correlate with the Federal Reserve’s benchmark interest rate.
Pros:
  • No risk of losing initial investment
  • Higher rates of interest than most savings accounts
Cons:
  • Penalities for using money before the CD Matures
  • Rate of Interest can be less than the current/future inflation rates

Treasury Bonds


Treasury bonds are like loans backed by the “full faith and credit of the United States” (aka the US Treasury). The government has never defaulted on its bonds, but bond prices can fluctuate. Bond prices and interest rates move in opposite directions, so when interest rates rise, bond prices fall.
Pros:
  • High Reliability/Security of Bond
  • Very liquid; can easily be converted to liquid cash
Cons:
  • Limited potential for growth
  • Bond prices will fall if interest rates rise

Corporate Bonds


Although real estate often requires more liquid cash, its potential return on investment is also greater. Great real estate can generate extra cash flow. Real estate also comes with its costs: property taxes, rent evasion, vacancy, damages to property etc.
Pros:
  • Wide range of issuers and interest rates to choose from
  • Less risky than owning the corporate's stock
Cons:
  • Higher risk of defaulting
  • Harder to liquidate into cash

Municipal Bonds


Municipal bonds can be viewed as bonds for local governments to help the government fund a projects like new roads and bridges. In return, they receive interest payments that are not taxed at any level.
Pros:
  • Comes with tax benefits along with an interest rate
  • Low volatility and high liquidity
Cons:
  • More beneficial for affluent investors
  • Interest rate and inflation risks
