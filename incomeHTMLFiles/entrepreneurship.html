Venture Capitalism


Venture capital is a form of private equity financing that is provided by firms or funds to startup, early-stage, and emerging companies that have been deemed to have high growth potential or which have demonstrated high growth.
Pros:
  • Time efficient and has the potential to generate lots of income
  • Large amount of ownership stake if venture is successful
Cons:
  • High costs to get involved in ventures and risk of losing everything
  • Very little data to rely on and make decisions

Sole Proprietorship/Partnerships


Sole Proprietorships and Partnerships are local businesses started by an individual or a group of individuals. The founders can pursue their passions while also earning money through the business.
Pros:
  • Complete control over business decisions and strategies
  • Easy to set up due to minimal paperwork and low filing fees
Cons:
  • High start-up costs and effort securing loans
  • Complete liability for all debt the business

Pros:
  • Opportunity for self employment and setting working hours
