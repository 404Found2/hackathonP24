Stocks

Index Funds/Mutual Funds


Index and mutual funds such as Vanguard allow for small investors to pool their money to create a common portfolio. The fund managers make decisions on behalf of all the stock holders. The fund keeps 1-5% for expenses.
Pros:
  • Diversified portfolios leading to less risk
  • Most funds can be trades on stock exchanges like NASDAQ and are very liquid
Cons:
  • Lack of control in choosing management and stocks inside of the fund
  • Low dividends as most earned money is re-invested

ETFs


ETFs, an abbreviation for exchange-traded funds, are like mutual funds for only one sector such as energies, cryptocurrency, or retailers. They only focus on one industry, which heavily influenced the price of the ETF.
Pros:
  • Low-cost and have a straightforward investing style
  • Less risky than owning the common stock of the individual companies
Cons:
  • Higher commission rates than normal stocks
  • Can be hard to liquidate based on where the stock is trading

Defensive Stocks/Non-cyclical Stocks


Stocks that tend to do well in any economic condition. These companies often sell essential items such as food, water, energy, medicine etc. Because their products are essential, these companies rarely face bankruptcy.
Pros:
  • Do well in any type of economic situation
  • Decreased (but still present) chance of losing initial investment
Cons:
  • Limited opportunity for high growth/returns
  • Tend to form price bubbles in times of recession

Dividend/Income Stocks


Dividend Stocks are stocks are stocks that have larger Dividend per Share Ratios. They act like bonds in the fact that they consistently give a portion of revenues back to investors, but also grow like normal stocks on the stock market.
Pros:
  • Higher dividends than most other stocks
  • Growth and treatment like a stock
Cons:
  • No guaranteed/fixed dividend amount
  • Increased taxes due to dividends being capital gains
