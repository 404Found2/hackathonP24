Royalties

Book Royalties


Book royalties are royalties paid out to authors for their books. Although in the past, publishing companies took a large share of profits, self-published authors tend to keep ~70% of all their profits.

  • High percentages of royalties with technological advances lowering costs
Cons:
  • Low success rates for authoring to be completely sustainable
  • Requires a lot of time and effort to create and advertise a book

Performance Royalties


Like book royalties, performance royalties are royalties paid out to experts for their talent. In this case, these experts are singers and artists being paid for their talent at singing and playing music whenever their songs are used.
Pros:
  • Larger potential customers that can buy the royalty
  • Increased awareness to lead to a positive feedback loop of more buyers interested in your songs
Cons:
  • Lower success rates without networks inside the industry
  • Understanding of legal considerations in the licensing agreements for royalties

Patent Royalties


Licensing intellectual property can promote a company's brand image, launch new products, and expand into more markets. Although this is something usually done by corporations, individual inventors can also profit from patent licensing.
Pros:
  • Break into new markets without a unique - uncopyable product
  • You don't need to invest capital to commercialize your product, this is done by the licensee as per the terms of the contract
Cons:
  • Patent holders only have the right to own a property for a limited period of time before others are able to replicate similar products
  • Liability for any damage the licensee does to the product or the image of your brand

Franchise Royalties


Especially lucrative for franchise owners of popular brands, franchise royalties are paid to businesses by individuals to use the brand name to generate income. Examples of companies with this type of business model are McDonald's and Dairy Queen.
Pros:
  • Larger royalty income for larger, well-known franchises
  • Control over your company as most franchise contracts allow the creator to retain control over aspects like marketing and inventory.
Cons:
  • Takes a lot of effort to grow a brand image and make it a franchise
  • Liability for any damage individual operators commit at their own locations

Mineral Royalties


A mineral royalty is a fee that is typically imposed by local, state and/or federal governments on either the amount of minerals produced at a mine or the revenue or profit generated by the minerals sold from a mine.
Pros:
  • Avoid some of the risks associated with ownership of the land
  • Immediate cash flow and a large initial/down payment
Cons:
  • Uncertainty in mineral deposits and future growth making it hard to predict fair prices
  • Potential tax consequences and other legal obligations
