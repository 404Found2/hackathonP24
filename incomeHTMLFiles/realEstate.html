Real Estate

Residential


Residential Real Estate consists of all land used for the purpose of housing. This includes buildings like condos, houses, and apartments. These types of investments provide a steady flow of income from tenants' rent.
Pros:
  • Steady monthly income on properties
  • Long-term capital appreciation that can also make it exempt from capital gains taxes
Cons:
  • Bad tenets can lead to eviction expenses and property damage
  • Property management can cost time and/or money

Commercial


Commercial real estate consists of the land used to operate businesses. This includes buildings like hospitals, restaurants and other stores. Instead of having tenants, it is businesses that rent out the area.
Pros:
  • Higher income and more reliable and professional customers
  • Less expenses as businesses are more likely to pay for expenses like taxes
Cons:
  • More expensive to enter into due to higher start-up costs
  • Need experience with local regulations when buying and maintaining properties

Industrial


Industrial real estate contains properties used for manufacturing, such as factories and warehouses. They can be found in various sizes and configurations, from small workshops to large distribution centers.
Pros:
  • Lower operating costs leading to more money for investor
  • Stable cash flows lasting several years without vacancy
Cons:
  • Zoning and location play large roles in getting/retaining tenets
  • Can take longer to fill vacancies when they arise

Raw Land


Raw land is land that has not yet been developed or prepared for construction. Usually, raw land consists of farmland, meadows, and woodlands. Even more than the other types of land, the location is key when buying raw land.
Pros:
  • Great appreciation rates and room for growth
  • Less competition and low maintenance costs
Cons:
  • Can be difficult to get proper financing
  • Zoning restrictions and easements on properties

Special Purpose


A special-purpose property is property that has limited utility other than its original purpose and is usually infeasible to convert it. These properties may include a hazardous waste facility, an oil refinery, or a specialized manufacturer.
Pros:
  • Long term rentals with credible clients
  • Advertisement money/revenue source in addition to rent
Cons:
  • Can be difficult to change the purpose of the utility
  • Hard to find potential buyers or renters
