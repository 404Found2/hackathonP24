Part Time Jobs

Part-Time Teacher/Tutor


Whether this can be tutoring or teaching people to play tennis, everyone has their own talents and teaching them can be a great side hustle. In addition to making money, teaching can also be an emotionally rewarding experience.
Pros:
  • Establishes a second career or income pathway in the case of an emergency
  • Potential to meet lifelong friends and worthwhile students
Cons:
  • Organizing the logistics can make starting cumbersome
  • Teaching can prove to be more time-consuming and lead to burn-out

Driver


Many companies such as Uber and Lyft are often hiring part-time drivers. Their platforms often offer ways for individuals to make extra money and are even advertised as the go to solution to make money.
Pros:
  • Less qualifications needed to apply and a large job market
  • Instant pay and bonus opportunities along with flexible hours
Cons:
  • Increased insurance and car/vehicle damage risks
  • Changing terms and conditions and reckless passengers

Freelancing


Because of the digital age, freelancing is a more and more common way to earn money. It consists of an individual self-employing themselves, choosing to work individually with clients and building up their clientele.
Pros:
  • Opportunity for self employment and steeper income growth rates
  • Freedom to work from any location and set working hours
Cons:
  • Uncertainty and unpredictability of finding new projects and clients
  • Ineligibility to receive employee benefits like paid time off or sick day

Seasonal Employment


Plenty of jobs run the course of a season, such as fall, winter, or summer, and can help you pay bills and keep busy. Examples include summer camp counselors, Santa and Easter Bunny costumes, and more!
Pros:
  • Great resume builder and opens up more earning opportunities
  • Extra money on a temporary basis without any long-term commitment
Cons:
  • Lasts only through the specific season the employment is available in
  • Lower wages and less employee benefits like paid time off and sick leaves
